What is a hackathon?

In the context of AI, a hackathon typically involves a dataset and a problem statement, using which the competitiors define AI models. The competitors are given a set period of time, wherein they create various AI models. The prediction of these models are compared with the actual data in the hands of organisers, to display errors/accuracy.

These errors/accuracies are then used to sort the competitors in a Leaderboard. At the end of the hackathon, the competitors at the top positions are awarded accordingly.

The platform we will use for our hackathon is kaggle. And in fact, many popular hackathons related to AI/ML take place here. A good place to start is This Notebook.

Why should you participate in a hackathon?

A hackathon is the closest thing to real life projects apart from, well real life itself. Sure the prelimnary hackathons like Titanic (Kaggle) and this, the Hackathon Uno are very static and there aren't many new things to learn after you learn the best solution(s).

But in more serious hackathons, where new solutions are required and a 'perfect solution' doesn't exist, The competitors get to learn a lot more than just various models and how they function, like context of various models, which model is appropriate, etc.

Hackathon Uno is designed not for you to specialize in AI/ML. Assuming you are in the DSAI degree, that will be taught to you anyways. The Hackathon has rather been designed to get you familiarized with AI/ML concepts with high-level usage of various concepts, readying you for more practical implementation of what you will be learning in due course.

What are the common challenges faced in hackathons?

You might find yourself yourself frustrated over deciding which model to implement. The notion of some models being 'better' than others is totally wrong. There is no perfect one-fits-all best model out there. You need to, instead of simply trying different models in a long tedious trial and error process; study various concepts and usability of models to improve your error margins/accuracies.
