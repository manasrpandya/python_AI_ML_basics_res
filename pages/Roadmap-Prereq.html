AI Learning Roadmap and Prerequisites

Learning Roadmap

Here is a step-by-step roadmap to guide you from scratch to participating in a preliminary hackathon:

  1. Learn Python Basics: Start with the fundamentals of Python programming. Focus on data types, control structures, and functions. (Time to allocate: few days to a week)
  2. Familiarize Yourself with Libraries: Learn to use essential libraries such as NumPy, Pandas, and Matplotlib. (Time to allocate: few days to a week)
  3. Explore Data Science: Understand data manipulation and visualization. Basically extrapolate what you learnt previously. (Time to allocate: few days to a week)
  4. Introduction to Machine Learning: Learn the basics of machine learning, including supervised and unsupervised learning. Try and define models applying your knowledge and working on a few small projects. (Time to allocate: few days to a week)
  5. Titanic! Test your knowledge by working on Titanic (Kaggle Competition). Lots of online resources are available on this particular competition and you can get assists from various different sources to get yourself exposed to the AI Competition/Hackathon environments. Make sure you iteratively submit code till you reach around 0.75-0.80 (75-80% accuracy), which is a good enough score for beginners, before trying to attempt the Hackathon Uno
  6. At this point you should have a strong enough knowledge base to attempt the Hackathon Uno.

Prerequisites

Before diving into AI, ensure you have the following prerequisites:

  • Basic knowledge of programming concepts.
  • Familiarity with mathematical concepts, especially statistics and linear algebra.
  • A working understanding of Python programming.
  • Access to a computer with internet connectivity for online resources and coding.
  • Willingness to learn and explore new technologies.
